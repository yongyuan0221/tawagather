# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed audience: store-floor employees (checking on a phone between shifts in a busy retail environment) and office/corporate staff (desktop, more relaxed context). The store-floor user is the harder constraint — glanceable, one-handed, fast to act on. Both groups share a single board; neither has an account.

## Product Purpose

Tawagather lets 99 Ranch Market employees discover, join, and host workplace events without any login or account. Sign-ups happen by selecting a name from a shared list. Success looks like: employee sees an event, taps join, is confirmed — in under 30 seconds, without needing IT or HR involvement.

## Brand Personality

Warm, approachable, team-forward. Like a well-designed bulletin board in the break room — not a corporate form, not a social feed. The 99 Ranch red anchors brand identity without feeling institutional.

Three words: **welcoming, clear, spirited**.

## Anti-references

- **Generic HR portals** (Workday, ADP, BambooHR): stiff, form-heavy, lifeless. Never feel like a compliance tool.
- **Social media apps** (Facebook Events, Eventbrite): engagement-maximizing, notification-driven, too "social network."
- **SaaS productivity dashboards** (Airtable, Notion, Asana): complexity-forward, overkill for a sign-up board, makes simple things feel hard.

## Design Principles

1. **Bulletin board, not portal.** Every screen should feel like something you'd post on a break room wall — information first, action obvious, no bureaucratic scaffolding.
2. **Glanceable by default.** Store-floor workers check between tasks. Key info (event name, date, spots left) must be readable at a glance without scrolling or expanding.
3. **Zero friction identity.** No passwords, no email, no account. Name selection is the one identity moment — make it feel natural, not like a login gate.
4. **Brand warmly, not corporately.** The 99 Ranch red is a team color, not a compliance badge. Use it to signal energy and belonging, not authority.
5. **One obvious action per screen.** Join, leave, create, share — never ask the user to figure out what to do next.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. 4.5:1 contrast for all body text and interactive labels. Mobile-first layout; comfortable touch targets (min 44px). No motion required for core functionality (animations are enhancement only).
